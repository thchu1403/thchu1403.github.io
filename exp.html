Customer Experience Center

A Life Insurance Company put efforts on designing their concept store and would like to bring out innovation ideas through customer behavior research. This would be the first concept store in Taiwan so the project is really tough and challenge. Our design team should work from zero to hero. Starting from UX research, co-create the in-store interaction scenarios with members from insurance company to very practical prototype making and service flow testing.

I took the responsbility of both being a ux researcher and designer. Start from developing the research plan, participating in the field study to hosting designing workshop and even designing the service flow within the store.

— UX Researcher, UX Designer

  • Contextual Inquiry
  • Field Study
  • Design Thinking Workshop
  • Wireframing
  • Service Design
  • Lo-fi prototype

Approach

Step 1

After conducting 10 street interviews and 6 in-depth interviews, my team debriefed Contextual Inquiry of the complex situation. Based on the result of the user study, we brought out different scenarios for different target audience cluster.






Step 2

The next thing we did is to host the design-thinking workshop in order to work together with our clients to bring out the solutions that fit different kind of our users and defining the “Hills” of the project core team. Within this stage, our client and I worked closely. I always remembered to hear their voice and put respect to them in order to know our client’s real needs.




Step 3

After discussing again and again, finally we selected our target audience and offered the digital solutions with user-centered design. We designed the interaction and made the prototypes of the service system on both the websites and the tablet.






Step 4

Besides, service design is another area that we put efforts on. We adjusted the interaction flow and also applied our knowledge of service design to make the interaction between each touch points reasonable and efficiency in order to provide a better user experience.






Step 5

Like the old saying-- “Practice makes perfect.” My team and I keep testing the service flow before the grand opening date and even helped our clients developing their staff training principles.

Employee Educations

After working with our clients for months, we delivered our design's value toward the whole corporation team including clients' internal members and vendors. I successfully improved my UX research skill through participating in the field study, interviews and survey design. In the concept store, we not only designed the service at every touch points but also tried to create a win-win service strategy for both the life insurance company and their vendors so that they can provide a user-friendly experience to the visitors together.
