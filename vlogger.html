Creator Behavior on Laptop

As content creation surged across platforms, HP sought to understand the specific workflow needs of YouTubers and vloggers — and identify what a best-in-class laptop experience would look like for creators. This research explored the distinct behaviors of video editors and live streamers across the North American and Chinese markets, surfacing device expectations, workflow pain points, and platform preferences that defined this audience — and pointing to product features and partnership opportunities that could differentiate an HP creator laptop.

I served as the UX researcher on this project, responsible for secondary research, participant recruiting, in-depth interviews, and synthesis. My findings gave the product core team a grounded persona framework and a set of hero experiences that shaped both product design decisions and strategic partnership discussions.

Approach

Step 1. Secondary Research & Market Mapping

The research began with a secondary study to map the content creator landscape across two target markets: North America and China. Creator behavior was segmented into two primary modes — video editing and live streaming — each with distinct platform ecosystems and tool dependencies. In China, key platforms such as Bilibili and Douyin shaped different hardware and connectivity expectations than their Western counterparts. This market mapping established the foundation for the recruiting strategy and interview protocol.

Step 2. In-Depth User Interviews

To ensure strong participant representation, follower count was used as the primary screening criterion — a segmentation approach that proved highly effective in surfacing consistent and comparable mindsets across creators. I conducted 6 in-depth interviews with creators across both markets, exploring their daily workflows, device setups, software stacks, pain points during editing and streaming, and unmet needs with current hardware. A consistent pattern emerged: creators' frustrations and priorities correlated strongly with their follower tier, validating the segmentation.

Step 3. Persona & Hero Experience Definition

Drawing on interview findings, I synthesized the data into 3 distinct creator personas — each capturing a different workflow model, device expectation, and aspirational outcome. These personas anchored the team's understanding of who they were designing for and what success looked like from a creator's perspective. I also defined a set of hero experiences — the moments where the right device could meaningfully reduce friction or unlock creative capability — that became the foundation for product direction and partnership evaluation.

Step 4. Strategic Partnership Engagement

With hero experiences defined, I worked with the product and business development teams to identify and evaluate third-party partners whose solutions could help deliver on the creator experience. I engaged with Canon to explore seamless camera-to-laptop file transfer — a critical friction point in video editing workflows — and with Adobe to align Premiere Pro's roadmap with the laptop's product cycle, ensuring compatibility for professional creators. Filmora was ultimately selected for pre-installation, offering accessible video editing tailored to this segment.

Strategic Partnerships

The research gave HP a creator-focused product strategy grounded in real workflow evidence. The persona framework and hero experience definitions informed hardware feature priorities and drove the strategic decision to pre-install Filmora as the default video editing application — a direct translation of research insight into product action.
